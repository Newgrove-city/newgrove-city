History of Newgrove

Early Settlement

Newgrove was not the technologically advanced and modern city that it is today. It started as an agricultural society, like most towns around the area. However, by the end of the nineteenth century, our town was declared a fishing town due to its privileged location near the Troutwater River and the Pacific Ocean.

Industrial Growth

From this point onwards, the town expanded in population and size as it turned into an industrial power in just a couple of decades. The incredible industrial developments made it easier and faster to produce and deliver commodities to other areas of the estate and the country, and made the town more affluent in general. Some of our most notable families in the present made themselves known during this period, such as the Ashcombes and the Brinewells, who have provided significant economic and cultural support to our society for decades.

Modern Era

Starting in the twentieth century, the city saw an influx of immigrants seeking job opportunities and a better life. In the latter half of the twentieth century and into the present, students from all corners of the globe have begun arriving to enjoy both a quality education and a lively city, evolving Newgrove into a college town. Despite this, it remains one of the most powerful fishing points in the estate, featuring an impressive dock area and an abundant source of river and sea fish.
