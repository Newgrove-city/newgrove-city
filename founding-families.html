Founding Families of New Grove

Along with our city's growth came our two main founding families: the Ashcombes and the Brinewells. Both families arrived in Newgrove around the same time, when Newgrove was becoming a fishing town, and they thrived from the lack of political and cultural unity and the underdeveloped fishing practices at the time.

The Ashcombe Family

The politicians and art patrons. The Ashcombes were not the first ones to notice the very different and divided political views regarding our town. Nonetheless, they were the first ones to act on it. They took advantage of the complex societal situation and began positioning themselves in positions of power, like the town hall. After some decades of hard work and publicity, the Ashcombe family managed to unify most of the citizens, urging them to leave behind economic differences and promote their cultural heritage as a common denominator for their harmony. Apart from politics, their other passion was art. They acted not only as a political force regarding state and national matters, but also as an economic force that promoted the arts through museums and education.

The Brinewell Family

The Silverwood family were renowned merchants who helped build New Grove’s market district. They funded the first bridge over the Silver River, connecting neighborhoods and promoting trade.
